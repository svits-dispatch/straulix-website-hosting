Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to ship a car?

The cost of shipping a car depends on several factors including distance, vehicle size and weight, transport type (open or enclosed), time of year, and route popularity. Short-distance shipments typically start around $300, while cross-country transport ranges from $800 to $1,500 or more. Use our instant quote tool for an accurate price.

How long does auto transport take?

Transit times depend on the distance and route. Local shipments (under 500 miles) usually take 1 to 3 days. Cross-country transport (1,000 to 3,000 miles) typically takes 5 to 10 days. Expedited shipping options are available for faster delivery.

What is the difference between open and enclosed transport?

Open transport uses an open-air carrier, which is the most common and affordable option. Your vehicle is exposed to weather and road debris but is safely secured. Enclosed transport uses a fully covered trailer that shields your vehicle from all elements, making it ideal for luxury, classic, or high-value vehicles.

Is my vehicle insured during transport?

Yes. All carriers in our network carry cargo insurance that covers your vehicle during transit. We verify insurance documentation before assigning any carrier. You will receive proof of insurance and a Bill of Lading before pickup.

How should I prepare my vehicle for shipping?

Remove all personal belongings from the vehicle. Keep the fuel tank at about one-quarter full. Disable any toll transponders and aftermarket alarms. Note any existing damage and take photos for your records. Ensure the vehicle starts and runs so it can be loaded onto the carrier.

Can I track my vehicle during shipping?

Yes. Once your vehicle is picked up, you will receive tracking updates so you can monitor the progress of your shipment. Our support team is also available to provide status updates at any time during transit.
